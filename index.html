About Institute

Gramonnati Mandal’s Agricultural Education journey began in 1990 with the establishment of Vidyamandir Agriculture Department. This initiative was guided by Shri K. C. Meher, Shri Anantrao Kulkarni, Dr. M. N. Dange, and Shri A. G. Meher, who laid a strong academic and infrastructural foundation.

In 1991–92, a one-year Gardener Training Certificate Course was started with the approval of Mahatma Phule Krishi Vidyapeeth, Rahuri. This marked the formal beginning of agricultural education at Gramonnati Mandal. The training centre received statewide recognition through a Doordarshan Mumbai programme titled “The Path of Development”.

In 1993–94, Yashwantrao Chavan Maharashtra Open University granted recognition to the Open Agricultural Education Centre, enabling students from deprived sections of society to pursue agricultural education.

Further expansion took place in 1995 with the approval of a two-year Marathi-medium Agricultural Diploma College by MPKV, Rahuri, creating wider access to agricultural education for rural youth.

In 2012, Gramonnati Mandal established the Agriculture Polytechnic under the Agricultural Technology Diploma scheme. The institute soon secured a position among the top ten agricultural polytechnics in Maharashtra.

In 2019, a fully English-medium Agriculture Polytechnic (Three Years – Six Semesters) was approved by the Government of Maharashtra. Students from this programme are eligible for direct admission to the second year of B.Sc. Agriculture. From 2015 to 2025, 91 students successfully qualified for degree programmes.

The Livestock Management & Dairy Production Diploma was introduced in 2002 with MAFSU, Nagpur approval. Since then, the programme has consistently received ‘A’ grade and has been recognized as an ideal college at the divisional level.
